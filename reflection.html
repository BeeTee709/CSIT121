Lessons Learned

Reflection on the project, testing, and the course as a whole.

Self-Reflection on the Final Project

Building this final project forced me to bring together everything we covered in CSIT121: semantic HTML, external CSS, layout, accessibility, and responsive design. One of my biggest challenges was organizing all of the labs into a single, cohesive site instead of treating them as separate assignments. Once I committed to a consistent layout and color scheme, the project started to feel more like a real portfolio and less like a checklist.

I also had to balance time pressure with code quality. That meant focusing on clear structure, valid markup, and a simple but readable design instead of trying to add every possible visual effect. In the process, I was reminded that good web design is less about decoration and more about clarity, hierarchy, and usability.

Testing on Multiple Browsers and Devices

I tested this site in at least two desktop browsers and on a mobile device. On desktop, the layout looked consistent in both Chrome and Firefox, but I noticed small differences in default font rendering and scroll behavior. Using a reset-like approach and explicit font choices helped keep things visually aligned.

On mobile, the media query was essential. Without it, the navigation and hero layout felt cramped and hard to read. After adjusting the layout to a single column and spacing out the navigation, the site became much easier to use on a phone. This reinforced how important it is to design with smaller screens in mind from the beginning.

Overall Course Takeaways

Throughout CSIT121, I learned how much power there is in the basics: semantic HTML, thoughtful CSS, and accessible structure. Understanding headings, landmarks, and proper use of lists, tables, and forms made it easier to build pages that are both readable and screen-reader friendly. The accessibility-focused labs also changed how I think about color contrast, alt text, and keyboard navigation.

The course also gave me a foundation for continuing to learn on my own. I now feel more comfortable reading documentation, experimenting with layouts, and debugging validation errors. This final project is just a starting point, but it represents a real step toward building professional, user-centered websites.
