Lessons Learned

Reflection on the project and the course as a whole.

Self-Reflection on the Final Project

Building this final project forced me to organize and truly learn the material from the course. Once I committed to a consistent layout and color I felt things really coming together. I think the dark theme and simple color makes for a readable and midly visually interesting design. I tried to follow the CRAP principles of design and I beleive I did a decent job.

During the development process, I was reminded that good web design is less about decoration and more about usability. Although it doesnt hurt to be pretty.

Testing on Multiple Browsers and Devices

I tested this site in edge and chrome but I noticed small differences I am happy I did because I found that one of my pictures was not loading at all. It may have taken me 3 hours to figure it out but we got it done. Using a reset-like approach and explicit font choices helped keep things visually aligned.

On mobile, the media query was essential. Without it, the navigation and hero layout felt cramped and hard to read. After adjusting the layout to a single column and spacing out the navigation, the site became much easier to use on a phone. This reinforced how important it is to design with smaller screens in mind from the beginning.

Overall Course Takeaways

Throughout CSIT121, I learned that development is a long process. Now that I am typing it out it sounds obvious with the name, But I think I had the misconception that web development was a quicker process. It takes time to get things right and to iterate on your ideas and having a plan of attack helps a bunch. One problem I run into developing is attempting to fix something and giving up early because I am not sure how to do it.

I think the most valuable idea that I will take away from this course is the importance of file managment and organization. I have made the mistake so many times of throwing files in a folder and calling it a day. This class is one that it will bite back for. Pushing to git is a nightmare when you are searching 10 different locations for your files.

Overall, Its cool to turn in a project that took so much time and effort. I have liked the class and the knowledge I have gained. I am dissapointed in myself for my performance in this class grading wisem but I do feel like I have learned a lot.

Thanks professor Noone!
